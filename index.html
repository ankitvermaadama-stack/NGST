National Symposium on Glass Science & Technology

NSGST-2026

28 February 2026 | Kanagala, Belagavi, Karnataka

Bridging glass science, technology, and industry to support innovation, sustainability, and self-reliance in India’s glass ecosystem.

About the Symposium

Glass is a critical material underpinning strategic sectors such as renewable energy, construction, automotive, pharmaceuticals, and advanced electronics. With India’s rapid infrastructure growth and large-scale solar deployment, the demand for high-performance and specialty glass is increasing at an unprecedented pace.

Despite strong academic research and growing industrial capacity, a visible gap exists between laboratory-scale innovation and large-scale industrial implementation. The National Symposium on Glass Science & Technology (NSGST-2026) is conceived as a focused national platform to bridge this gap through structured interaction between researchers, academicians, technologists, and industry professionals.

Organizing Institution

Gold Plus Group is India’s leading fully Indian-owned float glass manufacturer and a pioneer in architectural, automotive, and solar glass. With over one million tonnes of annual capacity and state-of-the-art manufacturing facilities, Gold Plus strongly advocates sustainable manufacturing, technological self-reliance, and R&D-driven growth.

The Kanagala plant in Karnataka, certified as an IGBC Platinum Green Factory, provides an ideal industrial–research ecosystem for hosting a national-level technical symposium.

Objectives

  • Dissemination of recent advances in glass science and technology
  • Strengthening interaction between academia, research institutions, and industry
  • Discussion on challenges in melting efficiency, defect control, refractories, and sustainability
  • Encouragement of young researchers and industry professionals
  • Identification of future research directions and collaborations

Scope & Themes

  • Glass physics, chemistry, structure, and properties
  • Furnace design, glass melting, and thermal processing
  • Refractories and industrial glass issues
  • Coatings, surface reactions, and corrosion
  • Strengthening and advanced processing of glass
  • Automotive, solar, pharmaceutical, and specialty glasses
  • Glass-ceramics, sol-gel glasses, and nanoglasses
  • Modeling, simulation, and numerical approaches
  • Glass manufacturing and processing machinery & equipment

Target Participants

  • Academic scientists and faculty members
  • Researchers and research scholars
  • Industry professionals and technologists
  • Engineers and R&D personnel
  • Students specializing in materials science, ceramics, and glass technology

Expected Outcomes

  • Stronger industry–academia collaboration
  • Exchange of best practices and emerging technologies
  • Collaborative research and consultancy opportunities
  • Capacity building and skill development
  • Support for India’s goal of self-reliance in glass manufacturing
